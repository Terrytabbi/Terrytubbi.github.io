Environmental Challenges

The Earth is facing numerous environmental challenges, such as deforestation, climate change, lack of clean water, and plastic & waste pollution. These environmental challenges poses a significant risk to our lives , the ecosystems and the overall stability of our societies. To protect our planet and our future generations, it is important that we acknowledge and address these challenges head on, pursuing new progressive solutions and adopting sustainable practices.

Deforestation

Deforestation is the permanent removal or destruction of forests. Its impacts include affecting the environment, causing soil erosion, disrupting water cycles, affecting livelihoods and contributing to climate change.

More Info

Water Scarcity

Water scarcity refers to the water demand exceeding the available supply in a local area or population. Its impacts include the destruction of habitats, loss of biodiversities, poverty, sanitation issues and diseases.

More Info

Global Warming

Global warming refers to the increase of the Earth's average surface temperature due to the accumulation of the greenhouse gases in the atmosphere. Its impacts include hotter temperatures, increased drought and rising sea levels.

More Info

Food Wastage

Food waste refers to the discarding or wasteful disposal of edible food. Its impacts include the production of methane - more harmful than carbon dioxide and using a lot of energy and natural resources to dispose of the food waste.

Use reusable bags, containers, and utensils

Using reusable items can reduce the amount of waste that ends up in landfills and the ocean. Using reusable items can also save money over time as we don't need to buy disposable plastic cutlery.

Reduce, Reuse, Recycle

Reducing can lessen the amount of waste that ends up in landfills and the natural environment.
Reusing gives you an alternate solution to some problems u might have at home. For example, a food jar can be washed and reused as a flower pot.
Recycling encourages the collection and processing of materials that we have used and turn them into new materials for others.

Use energy-efficient light bulbs and appliances

Use energy-efficient light bulbs, appliances, and electronics to reduce energy consumption. Energy-efficient light bulbs are designed to use less energy. Reducing energy consumption means that you indirectly reduce the demand for electricity which is produced from natural resources such as coal and natural gas.

Plant a tree (or two)

Greenhouse gas emissions continue to rise and the most natural way to combat it is by planting a tree. There are organizations in Singapore such as The Plant-A-Tree Program and National Parks Board.

Using Eco-friendly products

Use your purchasing power and make sure that your money is going towards positive changes. By supporting evo-friendly products, you're encouraging companies to source and produce their produces more sustainably and reduce the damage to the environment

My three personal goals

1. Educate others

Saving our Earth is a crucial issue that requires everyone's participation to achieve the desired results, therefore educating others on how to save the Earth is an important step towards creating a sustainable future. When I say I want to educate others, I don't just mean to raise awareness. It is about encouraging others to take action! How I would encourage others to take action is by providing them with practical tips on reducing their carbon footprint. For example, I can suggest using reusable bags, or turning off lights when not in use.

2. Reducing food waste

As someone that doesn't eat much, I tend to have some leftovers after meals. Knowing how much food waste is in Singapore (an average of 1.5kg of food is disposed of by each household in Singapore daily), I knew that I had to do my part to stop food wastage. I will start to plan the amount of food that I need to buy instead of mindlessly ordering food. This will prevent any wasted food as I only eat what I can and will order more when needed.

3. Conserving water

Water, it is an essential part of saving our Earth. Freshwater is a limited and valuable resource, conserving it will help to reduce our impact on the environment, conserve energy, and save money on our water bills. Some ways I will conserve water at home are using rain water instead of tap water to water my plants, fixing leaks coming from pipes, faucets, and toilets regularly, and installing water-efficient fixtures to save water bills and reduce my environmental impact.
